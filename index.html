What condition tells us about pricing
Updated homes — what they achieved
$2,000–$2,050
The two updated/good condition comps (13140 & 13343 Summerton, 14572 Laguna Beach) all leased at or above $2,000 at 100% of asking price. No negotiation required. The market recognized the condition and paid for it. Notably, 13343 Summerton — dated but clean — leased fastest of all at just 13 days and achieved the highest price at $2,050, suggesting demand in this submarket is present when a home is priced right regardless of finish level.
Dated home — what happened
$1,895 (asked $1,950)
1317 Lucaya Cir — dated but clean — was the only comp that did not lease at asking price. It required a reduction from $1,950 to $1,895 and took 54 days. The 97.2% SP/LP ratio is the only one below 100% in the dataset. This is the floor — and it belongs to the least updated property in the comp set. The subject at 1212 Coral Coast is updated, which clearly separates it from this outcome.
Key insight: The active listing at 14772 Laguna Beach Cir has been asking $1,900 for 61 days with no tenant — below every leased comp in the dataset. This suggests that pricing below $1,950 does not accelerate leasing; it simply signals condition concerns to prospective tenants. The subject property should not be priced defensively.

Listing strategy recommendation
List at $2,000. This is the market median, supported by two leased comps at exactly this price (100% SP/LP), and aligns with the updated condition of the subject property. It positions 1212 Coral Coast above the dated-home floor ($1,895) while avoiding the risk of the upper boundary ($2,050) where there is only one data point. At $2,000, demand has proven itself — twice — in this exact submarket within the past 90 days.
Consider $2,050 only if the interior finishes are meaningfully above the Summerton Drive comparables — for example, recent kitchen remodel, updated baths, or new flooring throughout. At that level there is one supporting comp but it is a dated home that moved very quickly, suggesting strong underlying demand. Test the market at $2,000 first; if strong interest materializes in the first 10–14 days, the owner retains the option to hold firm or adjust upward on the next lease cycle.
Do not price below $1,950. The active listing at $1,900 has been sitting 61 days — longer than every leased comp in the dataset. Pricing below the leased median signals condition concerns to prospective tenants and does not accelerate leasing. The data is clear: this market rewards updated homes priced at $2,000 with fast, full-price leases.
